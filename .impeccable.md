## Design Context

### Users
Live-coding musicians and audio developers experimenting with moondsp's mini-notation REPL in a browser. Usage context: sitting at a desktop or laptop, often in a dimly-lit studio or bedroom; occasionally on a tablet. Primary job: type rhythmic/melodic patterns and hear them update instantly. Secondary: discover available syntax from the cheatsheet.

### Brand Personality
**warm · mechanical · opinionated**

The engine is precise and technical (compiled DSP graph, rational time arithmetic), but the aesthetic should feel warm and hand-crafted — like a vintage synthesizer manual or an analog tape reel label. Not cold or clinical. Not try-hard retro. Grounded and maker-y.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Palette**: warm paper (`#f3efe5`) background, terracotta accent (`#b44f2c`), dark ink (`#1d1b18`). OKLCH-tinted; no pure black/white.
- **Dark mode**: deep warm brown (`#1c1814`) background, muted amber accents (`#e0784a`) — still warm, just darker.
- **Typography trio**:
  - Display: **Redaction 35** (slab serif, single weight — headlines only)
  - Body: **Atkinson Hyperlegible** (high-legibility sans — designed for readability)
  - Mono: **JetBrains Mono** (code / editor / dt labels)
- **Theme**: prefers dark experience but the light mode is primary (studio monitors usually face warm room lighting). Both modes are warm-tinted.

### Design Principles
1. **Legibility over decoration** — the editor is the product; chrome serves it.
2. **Warm, not cold** — tinted neutrals, organic palette, no pure grays or stark white.
3. **State is obvious** — audio status, parse results, and diagnostic squiggles communicate unambiguously without overwhelming.
4. **Touch-friendly by default** — 44px targets, sufficient spacing; the REPL is usable on a tablet.
5. **Motion is purposeful** — page-load reveal only; interactions are instant (<150ms) with gentle feedback. No bounce, no elastic.

### Anti-references
- Pure gray `#f5f5f5` backgrounds (too lifeless)
- Purple-to-blue gradients (AI aesthetic)
- Glassmorphism cards with glow borders
- Side-stripe borders on callouts (banned pattern)
- Gradient text (banned pattern)
